# Read Interviw Prep Initial Screening
Based on your resume and the interview feedback from previous candidates, this interview sounds like an **initial screening**. The interviewers are likely evaluating:

* Can you communicate clearly with business leaders?
* Have you worked in global enterprise teams?
* Do you have the technical depth for the role?
* Can you fit into ExxonMobil's collaborative engineering culture?
* Do you have experience in banking/trading systems that are similar to commodity trading systems?

Since you have **25+ years of enterprise experience**, your goal is **not to impress with every technology**, but to demonstrate that you've led enterprise projects, communicated with stakeholders, and can solve business problems.

---

# 1. Tell me about yourself (60–90 seconds)

This is almost guaranteed to be the first question.

### Talking Points

> "I'm a Senior Full Stack Engineer with over 25 years of experience building enterprise applications across the financial services, banking, healthcare, retail, and oil and gas industries. My primary technologies are Java, Spring Boot, React, Angular, Node.js, Python, AWS, and microservices.
>
> Most recently at Energy Transfer, I worked on modernizing internal enterprise applications by developing Spring Boot microservices, Angular applications, reporting solutions with SQL Server and SSRS, and deploying services on AWS EKS using CI/CD pipelines.
>
> Before that, I worked on several banking and financial platforms, including American Express, BNY Mellon, COBank, and most recently a financial inclusion platform where I designed secure APIs integrating multiple banking systems using Spring Boot, AWS, and event-driven microservices.
>
> What I enjoy most is partnering with business users to understand their needs, designing scalable solutions, mentoring developers, and delivering production-quality software."

# 7. How do you approach technical problems?

Excellent senior question.

### Talking Points

> "I always start by understanding the business objective before proposing a technical solution.
>
> Once I understand the requirements, I identify constraints such as scalability, security, performance, maintainability, and delivery timelines.
>
> I prefer building simple, maintainable solutions first and then optimizing based on measurable data rather than assumptions.
>
> Throughout development I keep testing, monitoring, and operational support in mind."

This answer demonstrates senior-level thinking.
